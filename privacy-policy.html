# DNSpector Chrome Extension Privacy Policy **Last Updated: May 4, 2025** ## Introduction This Privacy Policy explains how DNSpector ("we", "our", or "us") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our Chrome extension. We are committed to ensuring the privacy and security of your data while providing valuable DNS inspection functionality. ## Information We Collect DNSpector collects the following information: 1. **Domain Names**: We collect the domain names of websites you visit when you actively use the extension to analyze DNS records. 2. **DNS Records**: We retrieve DNS records (such as A, AAAA, MX, TXT, NS, SOA, CNAME, etc.) associated with domains you choose to analyze. 3. **Technical Data**: We collect basic technical information required for the extension to function properly, including: - Browser type and version - Extension settings and preferences - Logs of extension usage and performance 4. **Usage Data**: We anonymously collect data about how you use the extension, including: - Features used - Frequency of use - Error occurrences ## How We Use Your Information We use your information for the following purposes: 1. **Providing Core Functionality**: To analyze and display DNS records for the domains you request. 2. **Improving the Extension**: To understand how users interact with the extension and identify areas for improvement. 3. **Technical Support**: To diagnose and resolve technical issues when reported. 4. **Extension Performance**: To monitor and optimize the extension's performance. ## Data Storage and Security - **Local Storage**: Most data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage API and is not transmitted to our servers. - **Temporary Processing**: When you analyze a domain, DNS queries are processed temporarily to display results. - **Security Measures**: All data transmissions between the extension and DNS resolver services use secure HTTPS connections. - **Data Encryption**: Any sensitive data handled by the extension is encrypted using industry-standard encryption methods. ## Data Sharing We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. However, we may share data in the following limited circumstances: 1. **DNS Resolution Services**: To provide core functionality, DNS queries are sent to public DNS resolver services. 2. **Service Providers**: We may use third-party service providers to help us operate our extension, who may have access to your information solely to perform specific tasks on our behalf. 3. **Legal Requirements**: We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid legal processes. ## Your Choices and Rights You have the following rights and choices regarding your information: 1. **Access and Control**: You can access and delete locally stored data through your browser's extension settings. 2. **Opt-out of Analytics**: You can disable anonymous usage statistics collection in the extension settings. 3. **Uninstallation**: You can uninstall the extension at any time, which will remove all locally stored data. ## Data Retention We retain your data only for as long as necessary to provide the extension's functionality and for the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy. Most data is stored locally on your device and is deleted when you uninstall the extension. ## Changes to This Privacy Policy We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date. ## Contact Us If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at: Email: [your-email@example.com] ## California Privacy Rights If you are a California resident, you have specific rights regarding your personal information under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). These include the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete your personal information, and the right to opt-out of sales of personal information. ## European Privacy Rights If you are a resident of the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain data protection rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). These include: - The right to access, update, or delete your personal information - The right to data portability - The right to restrict or object to our processing of your personal information - The right to withdraw consent at any time ## Children's Privacy Our extension is not intended for children under 13 years of age.
